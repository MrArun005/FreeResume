# 📸 How to Capture Perfect Screenshots for LinkedIn

To make your post pop, you need high-quality visuals. Here is a guide to capturing the best angles of your new app.

## 1. The "Hero" Shot (Landing Page)

- **Goal:** Show off the "Fresh Mint" theme and the main value proposition.
- **Action:**
    1.  Go to the [Landing Page](https://free-resume-1mrgn21sl-aruns-projects-ddbf518f.vercel.app).
    2.  Zoom out slightly (Cmd -) if needed to fit the "Hero" section and the "Interactive Demo" resume in one frame.
    3.  **Capture:** Take a screenshot of the top section (Navbar + Headline + Interactive Resume).

## 2. The "Editor in Action" Shot

- **Goal:** Show the clean, user-friendly editor interface.
- **Action:**
    1.  Click "Create My Resume".
    2.  Fill in some dummy data (or use the "Load Demo Data" if available, or just type "John Doe" and a few details).
    3.  Open the **AI Assistant** dropdown to show off the "Mint" colored buttons.
    4.  **Capture:** Take a screenshot of the entire editor view (Sidebar + Form + Live Preview).

## 3. The "Dark Mode" Flex

- **Goal:** Show off the premium dark mode.
- **Action:**
    1.  Toggle the Theme Switcher to **Dark Mode**.
    2.  Scroll to the "Features" or "Templates" section on the landing page.
    3.  **Capture:** Take a screenshot showing the glowing gradients and dark UI elements.

## 4. The "Mobile" View (Optional but Good)

- **Goal:** Show it works on phones.
- **Action:**
    1.  Resize your browser window to be narrow (like a phone).
    2.  **Capture:** Take a screenshot of the mobile menu or the stacked layout.

---

## 💡 Pro Tip for LinkedIn

LinkedIn allows you to upload multiple images as a "Carousel" (document) or just a gallery.

- **Gallery:** Upload 3-4 images directly. LinkedIn will arrange them in a grid.
- **Carousel:** Save your screenshots into a PDF to create a swipeable carousel (high engagement!).
